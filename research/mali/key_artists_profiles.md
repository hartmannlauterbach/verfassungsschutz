# Key Mali Musicians - Detailed Profiles

## Ali Farka Touré (1939-2006)

### Biography
- **Birth**: 1939, Timbuktu region, Mali
- **Death**: 2006, Bamako, Mali
- **Ethnicity**: Songhai/Fula heritage
- **Occupation**: Musician, guitarist, singer, songwriter
- **Family**: Father of Vieux Farka Touré

### Musical Style
- **Genre**: Desert Blues, Traditional Malian music, World Music
- **Instruments**: Guitar, vocals, njarka (single-string fiddle), njurkle (lute)
- **Innovation**: Blended traditional Malian music with blues influences
- **Language**: Primarily Songhai languages

### International Career
- **Global Recognition**: One of Africa's most internationally renowned musicians
- **Collaborations**: Ry Cooder, Taj Mahal, Toumani Diabaté
- **Grammy Awards**: Multiple wins and nominations
- **Albums**: "Talking Timbuktu," "Savane," "In the Heart of the Moon"

### Cultural Impact
- **Cultural Ambassador**: Represented Malian music globally
- **Tradition Preservation**: Maintained and evolved traditional styles
- **Influence**: Inspired generations of African and international musicians
- **Legacy**: Continued through son Vieux Farka Touré

### Notable Works
- "Savane" (2005) - Final album, posthumous Grammy winner
- "In the Heart of the Moon" (2005) - Collaboration with Toumani Diabaté
- "Talking Timbuktu" (1994) - Collaboration with Ry Cooder

## Oumou Sangaré

### Biography
- **Birth**: 1968, Bamako, Mali
- **Ethnicity**: Wassoulou heritage
- **Occupation**: Singer, songwriter, businesswoman
- **Title**: "The Songbird of Wassoulou"

### Musical Style
- **Genre**: Wassoulou music, Afro-pop
- **Themes**: Women's rights, social issues, love, politics
- **Vocal Style**: Powerful, emotive vocals
- **Language**: Bambara and French

### International Career
- **Global Recognition**: International touring artist
- **Awards**: Multiple awards including UNESCO Artist for Peace
- **Collaborations**: Alicia Keys, Beyoncé, Diams
- **Festival Appearances**: Major world music festivals globally

### Social Impact
- **Women's Advocacy**: Strong voice for women's rights
- **Social Commentary**: Addresses social and political issues
- **Cultural Preservation**: Maintains Wassoulou traditions
- **Business Ventures**: Hotel and agricultural investments

### Notable Albums
- "Worotini" (2009)
- "Djeremando" (1996)
- "Ko Sira" (1993)
- "Moussoulou" (1989) - Debut album

## Salif Keita

### Biography
- **Birth**: 1949, Djoliba, Mali
- **Heritage**: Direct descent from Sundiata Keita, founder of Mali Empire
- **Challenge**: Faced discrimination due to albinism
- **Occupation**: Singer, songwriter

### Musical Style
- **Genre**: Mande-based Afro-pop
- **Vocal Style**: Powerful, distinctive voice
- **Traditional Elements**: Incorporates traditional Mande music
- **Modern Fusion**: Contemporary pop influences

### International Career
- **Global Recognition**: "Golden Voice of Africa"
- **Style Icon**: Traditional Malian clothing and presentation
- **Collaborations**: Carlos Santana, Wayne Shorter
- **Awards**: Multiple international music awards

### Cultural Significance
- **Noble Heritage**: Direct line to Mali Empire founder
- **Cultural Ambassador**: Represents Malian culture globally
- **Advocacy**: Albino rights and social issues
- **Tradition Bridge**: Connects ancient heritage with modern music

### Notable Works
- "Mansan Ka" (2000)
- "Papa" (1999)
- "Soro" (1987) - Breakthrough international album

## Toumani Diabaté

### Biography
- **Birth**: 1965, Bamako, Mali
- **Family**: Griot family lineage (71 generations)
- **Father**: Sidiki Diabaté (kora master)
- **Occupation**: Kora player, composer

### Musical Style
- **Instrument**: Kora (21-string West African harp)
- **Genre**: Traditional Mande music, World Music
- **Technique**: Virtuosic kora performance
- **Innovation**: Traditional music with contemporary elements

### International Career
- **Global Recognition**: Leading international kora player
- **Collaborations**: Ali Farka Touré, Björk, Damon Albarn
- **Grammy Awards**: Multiple wins
- **Festival Circuit**: Major international festivals

### Cultural Role
- **Griot Tradition**: Maintains oral historian tradition
- **Cultural Preservation**: Teaches and preserves kora traditions
- **Innovation**: Expands kora's global appeal
- **Education**: Teaches kora internationally

### Notable Works
- "Kora" (2023) - Solo kora album
- "The Mande Variations" (2008)
- "In the Heart of the Moon" (2005) - With Ali Farka Touré

## Tinariwen

### Biography
- **Formation**: 1979, Tuareg refugee camps
- **Location**: Originally from northern Mali
- **Members**: Collective of Tuareg musicians
- **Background**: Political refugees and musicians

### Musical Style
- **Genre**: Desert rock, Tuareg traditional music
- **Instruments**: Electric guitars, traditional instruments
- **Themes**: Tuareg identity, political struggle, desert life
- **Language**: Tamashek

### International Career
- **Global Recognition**: International desert music ambassadors
- **Festival Circuit**: Major world music festivals
- **Album Success**: "Tassili" (2011) Grammy winner
- **Collaborations**: TV on the Radio, Nels Cline

### Cultural Significance
- **Tuareg Voice**: Represents Tuareg culture globally
- **Political Commentary**: Addresses Tuareg political issues
- **Cultural Fusion**: Traditional with modern rock
- **Desert Blues**: Pioneers of desert rock genre

### Notable Works
- "Tassili" (2011) - Grammy winner
- "Aman Iman: Water Is Life" (2007)
- "Radio Tisdas Sessions" (2002)
