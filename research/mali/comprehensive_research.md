# Mali Music - Comprehensive Research

## Historical and Cultural Context

### Geographic and Demographic Background
- **Location**: West Africa, landlocked country
- **Population**: Diverse ethnic groups with Mande peoples (Bambara, Mandinka, Soninke) making up ~50%
- **Other Groups**: Fula (17%), Gur-speakers (12%), Songhai (6%), Tuareg, Moors (10%)
- **Historical Legacy**: Ancient Mali Empire (c. 1230 to c. 1600) continues to influence music

### Musical Heritage
- **Primary Influence**: Ancient Mande Empire traditions
- **Cultural Significance**: Music deeply embedded in social, political, and cultural life
- **Oral Traditions**: Strong griot (jali) tradition of oral historians and musicians
- **Global Recognition**: Mali considered "undisputed powerhouse of African music on world stage"

## Traditional Music Characteristics

### Key Traditional Instruments
- **Kora**: 21-25 string West African harp
- **Ngoni**: Traditional lute
- **Balafon**: Wooden xylophone
- **Djembe**: Drum
- **Various percussion instruments**

### Musical Traditions and Styles
- **Griot/Jali Tradition**: Oral historians maintaining cultural memory
- **Wassoulou Music**: Popular style from southern Mali
- **Mande Classical Music**: Traditional formal styles
- **Nomadic Traditions**: Tuareg and Moor desert music

## National Music Development

### Post-Independence Era (1960s)
- **First President**: Modibo Keita supported state-sponsored orchestras
- **Regional Orchestras**: Created for all seven regions
- **Annual Festival**: "Semaines Nationale de la Jeunesse" (National Youth Weeks)
- **Notable Groups**: Orchestre Nationale A (first electric dance band)
- **Traditional Ensemble**: Ensemble Instrumental National du Mali (40 musicians, still operational)

### Political Changes (1968-1980s)
- **Second President**: Moussa Traoré discouraged Cuban music, favored traditional
- **Festival Evolution**: Renamed "Biennale Artistique et Culturelle de la Jeunesse"
- **Political Decline**: End of 1980s saw reduced government support

## International Mali Musicians

### Legendary Male Artists
1. **Ali Farka Touré (1939-2006)**
   - Style: Songhai/Fula guitarist
   - Legacy: "Desert Blues" pioneer
   - International Recognition: Global world music star
   - Family: Son Vieux Farka Touré continues tradition

2. **Salif Keita**
   - Background: Noble-born, faced discrimination for being albino
   - Style: Mande-based Afro-pop
   - Approach: Traditional garb and styles
   - Title: "Golden Voice of Africa"

3. **Toumani Diabaté**
   - Instrument: Kora master
   - Family: Son of Sidiki Diabaté
   - Recognition: International kora prominence
   - Tradition: Griot family lineage

4. **Vieux Farka Touré**
   - Heritage: Son of Ali Farka Touré
   - Style: Continues desert blues tradition
   - International: Active global performer

### Prominent Female Artists
1. **Oumou Sangaré**
   - Style: Wassoulou music
   - Themes: Women's rights, social issues
   - International: Global recognition
   - Influence: Fashion and cultural trendsetter

2. **Fatoumata Diawara**
   - Style: Singer and guitarist
   - Recognition: Grammy nominations (2)
   - International: Global touring artist

3. **Kandia Kouyaté**
   - Era: Prominent since 1980s
   - Presence: Radio, television, markets
   - Appeal: Moralizing lyrics, traditional embodiment

### Contemporary Groups
1. **Amadou et Mariam**
   - Members: Amadou Bagayoko and Mariam Doumbia
   - Style: Musical duo
   - International: World music circuit

2. **Tinariwen**
   - Background: Tuareg band
   - Style: Desert rock/traditional fusion
   - International: Global festival circuit
